What is COVID-19

COVID-19 is a viral respiratory disease caused by a newly identified coronavirus strain. The virus was identified in late 2019 after cases were first reported in China. It is believed that the virus was transmitted from an animal to human. Such transmission is known as zoonotic transmission. People with COVID-19 might not show symptoms till about 2 to 14 days.

Coronaviruses in general are diverse family of viruses that share some similarities in symptoms of the diseases they cause. They mostly affect the respiratory system and can cause any respiratory condition from mild common cold to severe pneumonia.

What are the Symptoms and How does COVID-19 Spread

Symptoms of COVID-19:

People with COVID-19 can have symptoms ranging from mild to severe illness. Fever or chills, fatigue, cough, headache, congestion or runny nose, diarrhea, nausea or vomiting, sore throat, muscle or body aches and shortness of breath or difficulty breathing. Severe symptoms that need emergency medical help include: Trouble breathing, persistent pain or pressure in the chest, new confusion, inability to wake or stay awake, and pale, gray, or blue-colored skin, lips, or nail beds.

How COVID-19 Spreads:

COVID-19 is a respiratory condition that can spread from person to person. It spreads mainly between people who are in close contact with one another. It spreads through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person sneezes or coughs.

Minimizing and Preventing the spread of COVID-19:

The best way to minimize the spread of COVID-19 is to recognize the measures advocated by various healthcare authorities such as CDC and Ghana Health Services. These include:

  • Isolation of infected people
  • Avoid crowded places
  • Recognizing the six feet rule
  • Wearing face coverings that cover the mouth and nose
  • Basic hygiene: washing hands, avoid face touching
  • Get vaccinated

Vaccines available to help fight COVID-19:

  • Moderna mRNA-1273 (Available in USA and other countries)
  • Pfizer/BioNTech BNT162b2 (Available in the USA and other countries)
  • Janssen (Johnson and Johnson) (Available in the USA and other countries)
  • Gamaleya Sputnik V (Available in Ghana and other countries)
  • Oxford/AstraZeneca (Available in Ghana and other countries)
  • Serum institute of India Covishield (Available in Ghana and other countries)
